## Understanding the Limits of Free Tiers

Free file transfer services are designed for basic use. Think sending a single PDF contract, a few JPEG photos, or a small document. They often have strict limits:

- **File Size Limits:** Typically 2 GB to 5 GB. This is fine for documents, but raw video footage, high-resolution photography, or large design assets quickly exceed this.
- **Transfer Speed:** Often throttled. Large files take significantly longer to upload and download, impacting your turnaround time.
- **Storage Duration:** Files might only be available for a few days, forcing you to re-upload if the recipient misses the deadline.
- **No Resumability:** If your internet connection drops (a common occurrence with Spectranet, Swift, or even MTN/Glo during peak hours), your upload starts from scratch. This is incredibly frustrating for large files.
- **Limited Support:** When things go wrong, you’re often left with FAQs or a long wait for email support.

For a sole proprietor sending a document once a month, this is usually fine. For a business, especially one dealing with media, design, or large datasets, these limitations quickly become costly in terms of lost time and potential client dissatisfaction.

## When Free Might Still Be Enough

It’s not always necessary to upgrade. Free plans are still suitable if:

- **You send files infrequently:** Once or twice a month, for small documents.
- **Your files are consistently small:** Under 1 GB.
- **Your clients are tech-savvy and patient:** Willing to deal with potential download interruptions or slower speeds.
- **Budget is extremely tight:** And the risk of a failed transfer is acceptable.
