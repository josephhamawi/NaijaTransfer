## The Problem with File Compression and Delivery

When you're delivering photos, especially RAW files, quality is paramount. RAW files contain all the data captured by the camera's sensor. They offer the most flexibility in post-processing, allowing for adjustments to exposure, white balance, and color without introducing artifacts. Compressing these files, or converting them to formats like JPEG for easier transfer, means discarding valuable image data. This is unacceptable for clients who have paid for a professional service and expect the highest quality output.

Common scenarios where file size becomes an issue:

*   **Wedding Photography:** Delivering hundreds of RAW photos, plus edited JPEGs.
*   **Event Videography:** Transferring raw footage or uncompressed HD/4K video clips.
*   **Graphic Design:** Sending large PSD, AI, or high-resolution print-ready files.
*   **Architectural Visualization:** Moving massive 3D renders and CAD files.

Many services offer free tiers, but these are often too small for professional work. WeTransfer's free tier caps at 2GB. While great for a single PDF or a few JPEGs, it's insufficient for RAW photos. Paid plans often come with higher limits, but as Adekunle found, international payment can be a significant hurdle.

## Why Nigerian Payment and Internet Matter

Nigeria's internet infrastructure, while improving, can still be unreliable. Power outages, fluctuating connection speeds, and ISP changes are common. A file transfer service that doesn't handle these interruptions gracefully will lead to failed uploads, lost work, and frustrated clients. Imagine uploading 50GB of photos for 10 hours, only for the power to go out, forcing you to start over.

Furthermore, international payment gateways can be problematic. Many Nigerian bank-issued cards are not enabled for international transactions. Even if they are, currency conversion rates and potential markups add to the cost. Paying $12 USD per month for a service that frequently declines your card is not a sustainable solution.
