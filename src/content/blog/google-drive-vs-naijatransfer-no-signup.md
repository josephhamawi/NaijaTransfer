### The Challenge of Recipient Signups

Platforms like Google Drive are powerful for cloud storage and collaboration, but their file transfer mechanisms often assume a certain level of user engagement. When you send a file via Google Drive, you typically have two options:

1.  **Specific Permissions:** You invite specific Google accounts to view or edit the file. This requires the recipient to have a Google account and be logged in. It's great for internal team collaboration but cumbersome for external clients.
2.  **Public Shareable Link:** You generate a link that anyone with the URL can access. While this avoids signups, it can feel less secure, and managing access (e.g., revoking it later) requires deliberate action. Furthermore, Google Drive's free tier has limitations, and its primary function is not dedicated file transfer.

For studios and agencies, this means potentially losing clients due to a frustrating transfer experience, or spending extra time guiding recipients through account creation. The goal is to deliver files, not to onboard new users.

### Google Drive: Strengths and Weaknesses for Transfers

Google Drive is a robust cloud storage solution. Its integration with Google Workspace makes it a natural choice for many businesses. However, when it comes to sending files to external parties who shouldn't need an account, its limitations become apparent.

**Strengths:**
*   **Familiarity:** Many people already use Google services.
*   **Integration:** Works well within the Google ecosystem (Docs, Sheets, etc.).
*   **Storage:** Offers 15GB of free storage shared across Google services.

**Weaknesses for Transfers to Non-Users:**
*   **Forced Signups:** Often requires recipients to log in with a Google account, even for simple file access.
*   **Link Management:** Public links can be less secure and harder to manage than dedicated transfer links.
*   **File Size Limits:** While paid tiers offer more space, the free tier's 15GB is shared and can be quickly consumed. Transferring large video files can quickly exceed free limits.
*   **Not a Dedicated Transfer Service:** Its core function is storage and sync, not direct, frictionless file delivery.

Imagine a photographer in Abuja sending 20GB of high-resolution wedding photos to a client in Port Harcourt. Using Google Drive might involve breaking the files into smaller chunks, dealing with upload interruptions, and hoping the client can navigate the login process. This adds unnecessary stress to what should be a simple delivery.
