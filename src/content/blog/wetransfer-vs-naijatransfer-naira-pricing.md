## WeTransfer Pricing: The Dollar Dilemma

WeTransfer offers a free tier with a 2GB file limit. This is fine for documents or small JPEGs, but for professionals handling video, photography, or design assets, it's often insufficient. 

The WeTransfer Pro plan costs $12 USD per month. For Nigerians, this translates to several problems:

*   **Card Declines:** Many Nigerian bank cards are not authorized for international transactions. This means the checkout process often fails, leaving users stuck.
*   **Virtual Card Markups:** Users might resort to virtual dollar cards, but these come with additional fees and markups, increasing the effective cost.
*   **Unpredictable Exchange Rates:** The Naira to USD exchange rate fluctuates, making the true cost unpredictable and potentially higher than anticipated.
*   **Foreign Transaction Fees:** Even if a card goes through, banks often charge foreign transaction fees, further inflating the price.

For a professional needing reliable file transfer, the $12 USD monthly fee, coupled with the payment hurdles, makes WeTransfer a frustrating choice.

WeTransfer's standard upload protocol can struggle with these interruptions. A dropped connection means starting over, wasting time and data.

NigeriaTransfer utilizes the TUS protocol for resumable uploads. This means:
