## The Challenge of Delivering High-Quality Wedding Photos

High-resolution photography, especially wedding photography, demands significant storage and reliable transfer methods. RAW files are large, often 20-50MB per image, designed to capture maximum detail and dynamic range. A typical wedding could easily generate hundreds of these files, quickly accumulating into tens or even hundreds of gigabytes.

The primary challenges include:

-   **File Size**: Over 600 RAW images will easily exceed 15 GB. Most standard online transfer methods are not built for this scale without significant limitations.
-   **Quality Preservation**: Any compression applied during transfer will degrade the image quality. For professional work, this is unacceptable.
-   **Internet Stability**: Uploading 15+ GB on Nigerian internet, whether MTN 4G or Spectranet fiber, can be interrupted by power outages or network fluctuations. A service that doesn't support resumable uploads means starting over from scratch.
-   **Client Experience**: The client needs to download their files easily, without creating new accounts or navigating complex interfaces.
-   **Payment Hurdles**: Many international services bill in USD, leading to virtual card markups, international transaction fees, or outright card declines for Nigerian users.

## Why RAW Files Matter for Your Clients

RAW files are the digital negatives of your photographs. They contain all the unprocessed data captured by your camera's sensor. Unlike JPEGs, which are compressed and processed in-camera, RAW files offer maximum flexibility for post-production. This is crucial for several reasons:

-   **Maximum Data**: RAW files retain the most image data, providing greater detail in highlights and shadows.
-   **Post-Processing Flexibility**: Clients or their chosen retouchers have more control over white balance, exposure, color correction, and noise reduction without introducing artifacts.
-   **Archival Value**: RAW files are future-proof. As editing software improves, older RAW files can be reprocessed to even higher standards.

Educating your clients on why you deliver RAW files (even if they also receive edited JPEGs) adds value to your service. For a deeper dive into the benefits, you can refer to [resources on RAW vs. JPEG](https://www.adobe.com/creativecloud/photography/discover/raw-vs-jpeg.html).

## Common Pitfalls: What Not to Do

Many common methods for sharing files are unsuitable for delivering large quantities of high-quality RAW images:

-   **WhatsApp**: While convenient for quick snaps, WhatsApp aggressively compresses images and videos. Sending professional RAW files this way will render them unusable for print or serious editing.
-   **Email**: Most email providers have strict attachment limits, typically around 25MB. Sending even one RAW file is often impossible, let alone 600.
-   **Google Drive or Dropbox (Recipient Experience)**: While useful for personal storage, asking clients to sign up for an account to download their wedding photos creates unnecessary friction. Public sharing links can also raise trust concerns for some recipients.
-   **WeTransfer Pro (for Nigerian Users)**: WeTransfer's free tier is limited to 2 GB, which is insufficient for a wedding delivery. Their Pro tier costs $12 USD per month. For many Nigerian photographers, this means dealing with international dollar billing, virtual-card markups, and the common frustration of local bank cards declining international transactions at checkout.

## Best Practices for Client Delivery

Beyond choosing the right platform, adopting best practices ensures a smooth delivery process:

-   **Organize Your Files**: Before uploading, create a clear folder structure. For example, `Client Name - Wedding Date - RAW Files`. This makes it easy for the client to navigate the downloaded content.
-   **Communicate Expectations**: Inform your clients how and when they will receive their files. Explain why RAW files are large and why your chosen delivery method maintains quality.
-   **Set Download Windows**: If your service has expiry dates for links, communicate these to your client. Encourage them to download and back up their files promptly.
-   **Maintain Your Own Backups**: Always keep multiple backups of your original RAW files, even after delivery. Hard drives, cloud storage, or a combination are essential.
-   **Data Privacy**: Ensure you are compliant with local data privacy regulations, such as the Nigerian Data Protection Regulation (NDPR), when handling client data. You can find more information on [NDPR framework on NITDA's website](https://nitda.gov.ng/ndpr-framework/).
