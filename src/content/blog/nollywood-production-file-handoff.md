## The Challenge of Large File Transfers in Nigerian Production

Producing films, TV shows, or even high-resolution photography in Nigeria often means dealing with massive data. Raw video footage from cinema cameras can easily hit hundreds of gigabytes per day. Photographers shooting professional shoots can accumulate tens of gigabytes of RAW files. The challenge isn't just the size; it's reliably getting these files from the set or studio to the editor, director, or client, often on a tight deadline.

Several factors make this particularly difficult in Nigeria:

*   **Internet Instability:** Power outages, fluctuating ISP performance (whether it's Spectranet, Swift, or even MTN/Glo mobile data), and inconsistent 4G/fiber speeds mean that traditional uploads can fail repeatedly. A 100GB transfer that takes 10 hours could be interrupted dozens of times.
*   **Payment Hurdles:** Many international services like WeTransfer, Dropbox, or Adobe Creative Cloud require USD payments. Nigerian bank cards often face international transaction blocks or require specific approvals. This leads to failed payments, especially with virtual cards or when using local debit cards for international services.
*   **Recipient Hassles:** Requiring clients or collaborators to sign up for new accounts (like Google Drive or Dropbox) adds friction. They just want the files, not another login to manage.
*   **Compression Walls:** Sending files through messaging apps like WhatsApp or Telegram inevitably leads to aggressive compression, degrading the quality of professional media. This is unacceptable for rushes where visual fidelity is critical.
